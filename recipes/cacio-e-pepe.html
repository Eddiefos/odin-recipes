Cacio e pepe

Description

Cacio e pepe is a classic Roman pasta dish that translates to "cheese and pepper," made with just three simple ingredients: pasta (traditionally spaghetti), freshly grated Pecorino Romano cheese, and coarsely ground black pepper. The magic lies in the technique – the starchy pasta cooking water is used to create a silky, creamy sauce that coats each strand without any cream or butter, transforming humble ingredients into one of Rome's most beloved comfort foods.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti
  • Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Whole black pepper

Steps

  1. Cook pasta until aø dente, reserve 1 cup pasta water before draining
  2. Toast peppercorns in a pan for 2-3 minutes, then grind coarsely
  3. Make sauce by whisking grated Pecorino Romano with cooled pasta water (about 150°F) until smooth
  4. combine hot pasta with cheese mixture, toss vigorously off heat until creamy
  5. Serve immediately with extra cheese and pepper

Key tip: Let the pasta water cool to 150°F before mixing with cheese to avoid lumpy sauce
